Alex Slomka

Alex Slomka is a trombonist located in the NYC Metropolitan Area whose work spans big band, jazz, and contemporary ensemble performance.
He holds a bachelor’s degree in trombone performance from the Crane School of Music and a master’s degree in jazz performance from the Purchase Conservatory of Music, where he honed his voice as a performer through intensive ensemble work, improvisation, and collaboration with artists across genres.
Alex has performed with numerous notable groups including The Birdland Big Band and Latin Jazz Big Band, The Stan Reuben Orchestra, and the Dan Pugach Big Band with Nicole Zuraitis, and CeCe Winans.
In addition to a busy performing career, he has recorded with many prominent artists including Debbie Wileman, Anna Wise, The Lemon Twigs, and Harmonic Aggression. Currently a member of the Especially Everyone Ensemble, Alex is known for his versatility, musicality, and collaborative approach, and remains an active performer in the greater New York City music scene.

Jason Winikoff Trio

Dr. Jason Winikoff is a drummer, percussionist, bandleader, and ethnomusicologist who recently relocated to Connecticut. Though born in New Jersey, music has taken him from Vancouver to Zambia and many places in between. The perpetual student, Winikoff dedicates his artistic practice to the locations and musical cultures that have shaped him. Having cut his teeth in New Orleans, he is especially drawn to the vast musical landscape of the Crescent City. As such, the Jason Winikoff Trio performs music of the often-overlooked second wave of New Orleanian jazz composers. This repertoire includes songs by Harold Battiste, Ellis Marsalis, James Black, and their contemporaries. Expect beautiful ballads, bouncy bop tunes, and driving modern compositions that challenge notions of a static New Orleanian sound. All the while recognizing the deep lineage these songs derive from, the Jason Winikoff Trio also frequently sneaks traditional jazz into sets - showcasing the diverse shapes this art form can take. Joining Winikoff are some of the region's finest jazz musicians.

EASTBOUND

EASTBOUND: born from the Norwalk and Weston music scenes — unites members of Juicebox (Norwalk, CT) and Caravan (Weston, CT) Jessie Herman [Guitar/ Vocals], Noah Benson [Guitar/ Vocals], Trevor McGee [Keys/ Vocals], Sam Bliss [Bass/ Vocals], and, Ben Jachimczyk [Drums] into a energetic live collective. With deep roots in the local circuit and a long shared history of jamming, EASTBOUND channels a love of spontaneous performance into psychedelic grooves, inspired reinterpretations of favorites, and free-flowing improvisation. Expect high-energy shows built around exploratory jams, and moments that take familiar songs into new, unexpected directions. Eastbound is music for listeners who crave the live, in-the-moment experience.

Kovac Brothers

Frank and Jim Kovac combine over 30 years of music experience and a craving for originality to create Kovac Brothers. The sound emerges with a courageous style of Rock, with a Country-Jazz Spice! Drawing on their unique experiences the brothers instinctively realized how to work towards making music that had mass appeal but was interesting enough to hold the attention of the most discerning listener. Their first album “Seeing is Believing” revealed a wide range of influences and their new material has displayed this trend with a bit more risk taking. Moreover, their interesting versions of various covers are creative and just fun, ranging from TLC, The Grateful Dead, Steely Dan, The Meters, Beyonce, and beyond. For every listener there is always a surprise about to be heard.
